What is human trafficking?

"Human Trafficking is a crime involving the exploitation of someone for the purposes of compelled labor or a commercial sex act through the use of force, fraud, or coercion." - Be The One | UTA

"Human trafficking is a modern-day form of slavery involving the illegal trade of people for exploitation or commercial gain." Utah Attorney General's Office

Types of trafficking

Sex Trafficking

Where individuals perform commercial sex through the use of force, fraud, or coercion

Top 5 Venues:
1.Online Ad. Venue Unknown
2.Residential Brothels
3.Pornography
4.Hotel/Motel Based
5.Truck Stop-Based


Labor Trafficking

Where individuals perform labor or services through the use of force, fraud, or coercion

Top 3 Venues:
1.Traveling Sales Crews
2.Domestic Work
3.Forestry/Reforestation



Sources:
Be The One | UTA

Who are the traffickers?

There is no one type or profile for a human trafficker. "They span all racial, ethnic, gender, and socio-economic demographics." They could be anyone.


Trafficker Tactics

  • Violence or threatening loved ones
  • Harm or deprivation of basic necessities
  • False promises
  • Restriction of contact to loved ones
  • Limitation of freedom of movement
  • Controlling personal identification documents
  • Threats of deportation or law enforcement action
  • Garnishing of victim's salary to pay off debts



Sources:
Utah Attorney General's Office
Be The One | UTA
